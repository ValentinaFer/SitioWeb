Raspberry Hand Pies!

These raspberry hand pies, also known as turnovers, are the perfect combination of sweetness of the raspberry filling and saltiness of the flaky crust. Ideal for picnic or as an afternoon treat.

Ingredients!

Servings: 12 hand pies

Directions!

These hand pies are made from two components: the pie crust and the raspberry filling. Of course you can use a pre-made pie crust if you like, just skip steps 1, 2 and jump right into making the raspberry filling.

  1. Prep crust:

    Start by whisking all the dry ingredients for the crust in a large mixing bowl. this will be fluor, sugar and salt.

    Next you want to cut the cold butter into small pieces and add it to the mixing bowl with the dry ingredients. It's very important that the butter is extremely cold because this will help you make the crust very flaky.

    Cut the cold butter through the dry ingredients until it's the texture of coarse meal. This will coat the flour in fat, keeping the crust very tender. The tiny pieces of butter will also melt in the oven and create all the flakiness in the crust.

    Now you are going to add in the ice cold water 1 TBSP at a time.

    You can use a fork to nudge the flour and fat into the water. Be careful not to aggressively stir or smash down on the mixture. When you can gather a clump of dough in your hand and it holds together, then you are ready to chill the dough.

  2. Chill the dought:

    Press the dough together and then into a dish. Wrap in plastic wrap and chill in the refrigerator for about 1 hour.

    Chilling the dought will allow the flour to completely hydrate and will allow the gluten time to relax. Also giving the dough time to firm up.

  3. Prep filling

    Wash your fresh berries and let them drain completely. The filling doesn't work well with frozen berries as they release a little too much liquid. If you don't have access to fresh berries, it's recommended to use raspberry preserves as filling.

    About 5 minutes before rolling out the dought, mix the berries together with the other filling ingredients. Be gentle so you don't break the berries. Also don't do this to early as the berries will start releasing their juices.

  4. Roll out pie dought

    Divide the dough into 12 fairly equal pieces. You can do this with a kitchen scale or just eyeball it.

    Roll the pie dough out, one piece at a time, into roughly a circle shape about 1/16th of an inch thick. You can also just roll out the dough and use cookies cutters instead./p>

  5. Fill dough

    Place about 2 TBSP of filling in the center of piece of dought and then fold it over and seal the edges. You can help yourself with a fork to seal the edges so they don't burst open in the oven. Brush egg wash on top of each hand pie and if you wish, you can add sprinkles.

  6. Bake

    Finally, bake the pies until nicely golden brown. Allow them to cool for several minutes before enjoying.
